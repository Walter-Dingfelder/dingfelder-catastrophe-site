Why 40 kHz Matters

Most people only hear up to about 20 kHz. Your coreless induction furnace doesn’t care about that limit. Its earliest warning signs live in the ultrasonic band, long before a human ear or handheld meter notices anything is wrong.

Fortune Teller™ listens to the full acoustic fingerprint of the melt — from deep coil hum to ultrasonic stress crackle — and indexes every pattern into the V.A.U.L.T™ for Predictive Breakpoint™ analysis.

Typical fault signatures and where they first appear:

  • Coil hot-spot discharge: ~28–32 kHz
  • Molten metal flash arc: ~30–40 kHz
  • Micro refractory crack: ~24–30 kHz
  • Cavitation ping (cooling circuit): ~22–28 kHz
  • Water leak hiss (steam / jacket breach): ~16–24 kHz
Human Hearing: roughly 20 Hz–20 kHz. Operators hear coil hum, pump noise, and the louder end of water leak hiss — but most early damage signatures sit just above this band. By the time a fault is clearly audible, it’s usually well advanced.
